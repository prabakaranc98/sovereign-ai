# Sovereign AI — What It Means to Own the Intelligence

*A living repo on sovereign AI and the frontier ecosystem — research + journaling + case studies, built in public over ~10 weeks.*

## Abstract

"Sovereign AI" gets used as if everyone agrees on what it means. A nation building its own frontier lab, an enterprise hosting an open-weights model on-prem, a startup fine-tuning on proprietary data, an individual running a local model on their laptop — all of these get called sovereignty, and they are wildly different bets with different costs, different failure modes, and different definitions of "owning" anything at all.

This repo is a 360-degree exploration of that question: **what does it actually mean to own the intelligence?**

- **For a nation** — is sovereignty a frontier lab, a compute reserve, a data regime, a talent pipeline, or a treaty position? What are countries actually buying when they announce "sovereign AI" programs?
- **For an organization** — is it a model hosted on-prem with access handed out to employees? A fine-tuned checkpoint? Control over the data flywheel? Or is it something closer to owning the *harness* — the agents, evals, and workflows built on top of someone else's model?
- **For a startup** — where is the real leverage: weights, data, distribution, or the layer of judgment encoded in prompts, tools, and product decisions?
- **For an individual** — a local model, a personal memory store, an agent that answers to you alone. Is personal sovereignty even coherent, or is it a romantic frame for a hobby?

The exploration runs the full altitude range — from the minute (a server, an SDK, an inference stack, a deployment pattern) to the systemic (how intelligence ownership composes inside an organization or a nation-state). Along the way it collects and stress-tests the bets and theses of the people shaping this debate: builders and lab leaders (Dario Amodei, Alex Karp, and their contemporaries), professors and scientists, journalists, and the people who care most about policy, governance, and safety — alongside the engineers, product leaders, founders, and investors who have to act on all of it.

**What this is not:** a roadmap, a tutorial series, or a link dump. The format is conversational — captured discussions, critical insights, identified patterns — then simplified and retold through multiple personas. The same question ("what does owning the intelligence mean?") should have a legible answer for an engineer, a life-sciences researcher, a policymaker, a founder, and a beginner. If an insight can't survive translation across those lenses, it probably isn't an insight yet.

## How this repo works

| File | Role |
|------|------|
| [README.md](README.md) | The abstract and orientation — what this project is and where it's going. |
| [SCRATCHPAD.md](SCRATCHPAD.md) | The raw workbench. All unfiltered thoughts, conversation notes, half-formed hypotheses, and reading trails land here first. |
| [INSIGHTS.md](INSIGHTS.md) | The distillery. Only insights that earn their place — worth telling other people — get promoted here from the scratchpad. High bar, deliberately. |
| [research-engineering.md](research-engineering.md) | The method. What "research engineering" means here: how sovereignty claims get earned through probes, builds, and measurements instead of asserted. |
| [CLAUDE.md](CLAUDE.md) | The collaboration contract. Portable working memory for any AI collaborator joining the project — the framing, rules, working mode, and live hypotheses in one file. |
| [src/](src/) | The lab bench. Probes, experiments, and system builds — each traceable to a scratchpad question that predates it. |
| [docs/](docs/) | The writing room. Where survey/landscape material and the white paper get composed from promoted insights and probe write-ups. |

Topics that mature in `INSIGHTS.md` will eventually expand into their own folders and deep-dives. Nothing gets its own folder until it has earned it.

## The 10-week immersion (rough shape)

Not a schedule — a commitment to depth. The plan is to inhabit different perspectives over the coming weeks: the engineer's, the researcher's, the leader's, the contrarian's, the beginner's. Each perspective gets to ask the sovereignty question its own way, and the answers get captured conversationally as they emerge.
