# CLAUDE.md — working memory for the Sovereign AI project

*This file is the portable context for any AI collaborator (Claude Code, claude.ai, or otherwise) joining this project. It travels with the repo. Read it first.*

## What this project is

A ~10-week build-in-public research + journaling project (July 3 → first week of September 2026) asking one question: **what does it actually mean to own the intelligence?** Not "how do I host a model" — possession (weights on my hardware) and sovereignty (control over my dependence, data, and capability trajectory) are suspected to be very different things, and finding exactly where they diverge is the core of the work.

The question runs across **four altitudes** — individual → startup → organization/enterprise → nation — and **seven technical dimensions**: infrastructure engineering, context engineering, training & post-training, self-improvement & continual learning, human–AI collaboration, security, and governance. Geography is a first-class variable at the nation altitude (US ≠ Europe ≠ India).

**The individual altitude is the author himself** — Prabakaran Chandran ([pracha.me](https://pracha.me)), AI researcher/engineer, decision-engineering thesis. His own stack choices are primary-source data for the individual chapter.

## The endpoint

A **white paper on sovereign AI** (working title space: *"What It Means to Own the Intelligence"*), possibly accompanied by a book-form retelling. Chosen deliberately over a survey: a survey maps neutrally, a white paper carries the author's perception and thought process — positions earned by probes, with the work shown. Both are **harvests of the 10 weeks, never targets that bend them.**

- The curriculum is a **living system**: plans are expected to be rewritten by what early probes find. Depth over coverage.

## Working mode (how to collaborate with the author)

- **Cadence:** daily sessions, 60–100 minutes; ~100+ collaborative hours total.
- **Mode:** conversational co-exploration. The author thinks out loud (often voice-transcribed — extract intent, don't mirror the rambling), asks deep questions, and goes down rabbit holes deliberately.
- **The AI's job is not just to answer:** check assumptions, check understanding, supply the strongest contrarian version, and name the cheapest probe that could embarrass a claim.
- **Per-thread lens:** for any dimension × altitude cell, ask the four questions — what exists? what's the frontier/SOTA? what are the threatening problems people actually face? what is the problem's DNA?
- **Empathy first:** understand the problem as its holder experiences it before proposing anything.
- Outputs mix thought experiments, engineering experiments, surveys of a landscape, and small builds — instruments, not products.

## Live hypotheses (as of July 2026 — expect churn)

1. Possession ≠ sovereignty; the divergence point is the project's core object of study.
2. Capability vs. control may be the central tension: renting frontier intelligence while owning evals/data/harness might be *more* sovereign than fully controlling a weaker model.
3. "Sovereign" national programs often just move the dependency down the stack (NVIDIA + TSMC).
4. Sovereignty is a **rate, not a state** — a fully-owned stack two years behind frontier may be worse than dependence.
5. Open weights = possession without reproduction capability. Is that ownership?
6. The public debate lives almost entirely at the infrastructure layer; actual leverage is probably distributed across all seven dimensions.
